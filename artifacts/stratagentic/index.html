Photography & visual storytelling

Gulliver's Travels.
The character lives in
the real world.

The photography direction is not a filter, not a prism, not an editorial portrait. It is this: an illustrated character coming alive as a miniature inside a real photographic environment. The world goes on around it — boardrooms, café tables, desks, city streets. The character is already there. You just hadn't noticed.

The defining image of this direction. A woman peers over a wooden surface at the miniature character below. Close crop. Shallow depth of field. The crossroads of reality vs the imaginary. Of opportunities. This is what stops a scroll.
Boardroom — the globe character sits at the far end of the table. The room is empty. It is already working.
Street / café — traveller on suitcases on a rain-slicked city ledge. The world rushes past. She is composed.
Desk / workspace — headstand character on a clean desk. Playful, surprising, unmistakably Stratagentic.
Play / build — the runner navigates a Lego landscape. Scale inverted. Human logic suspended.

The character is in your world.
Not in a screen.

Every other AI brand puts its imagery inside screens and interfaces. We put our character in physical space — on a desk, on a café table, in a conference room. Real photography. Real environments. Shallow depth of field. Macro or tight crop.

The result is intimacy and surprise. You have to look closely. The AI is already there — you just hadn't noticed it. A unique proposition in the worlds of consulting, tech, or strategy communications.

01
Physical surfaces only
Never on a screen. Never in a UI. Always on a real material — wood, paper, fabric, stone. The physicality is what makes it surprising.
02
Scale contrast is the concept
The miniature scale is the idea. AI part of the world. The work is happening all around you. You just have to look.
03
The shadow is non-negotiable
The character casts a shadow onto the surface. It marks the character as almost-real — intelligence in physical form, not a toy.

The governing principles

Guidelines

01
Filled. Bold. In character.
The new illustration set — filled forms with tonal shadow, strong personality — is the one to commission and deploy. When in doubt, go smaller. Expressive drawing style.
02
Choose the background aimed at audience
Dark, or sky blue signals premium ambition. Off-white (#F4F1EB) signals approachable intelligence. Both are correct. Neither is a compromise. Never mix them in one composition. Never use pure white or pure black.
03
The human is always at ease.
No stressed characters. No urgency. No reactive postures. The entire promise of Stratagentic is that your organisation can breathe while the AI runs. If an image shows a human reacting to technology, it breaks the brand truth.
04
Photography = character in your world.
No prisms. No portraiture. The only approved photographic direction is Gulliver's Travels — the illustration placed as a miniature in real physical environments, with a shadow.
